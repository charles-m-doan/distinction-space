The proposal is coherent, clearly motivated, and targets a genuine fault line in contemporary metaphysics. It articulates a **minimal ontological constraint** rather than a substantive ontology, which is a strength. The core idea—that facthood requires a contrast between obtaining and not obtaining—captures an intuition many metaphysicians rely on implicitly but rarely state explicitly.

That said, the proposal is not uncontroversial. Its force depends on how several key notions are understood. What follows is a structured assessment.

---

## What the MFC successfully establishes

### 1. It isolates a genuinely metaphysical constraint

The MFC is not epistemic, semantic, or pragmatic. It concerns **what actuality must be like** for there to be facts at all. This avoids common deflationary replies that shift the issue to language or knowledge.

### 2. It blocks vacuous ontology

The argument correctly identifies a real risk in permissive or deflationary ontologies: if existence imposes no constraints, then ontological commitment collapses into stipulation. The MFC rules this out at the lowest possible level.

### 3. It explains why “existence” must do work

The proposal clarifies why metaphysical discourse presupposes a contrastive notion of being. Without some minimal distinction between being and not being, the subject matter evaporates. This is a legitimate transcendental-style argument.

### 4. It is genuinely minimal

The constraint does not assume plurality, causation, spatiotemporality, or structure. It requires only **non-identity between actuality and non-actuality**, which makes it difficult to reject without radical consequences.

---

## Where resistance is likely to arise

### 1. Ambiguity in “makes a difference”

The phrase “makes a difference, even in principle” invites interpretation. Critics may ask:

* Difference to *what*?
* Difference in *which respect*?

If “difference” is understood robustly (e.g., causal, modal, explanatory), the constraint risks excluding views it may not intend to exclude. If it is understood thinly, critics may argue that the constraint becomes trivial.

Clarifying that the difference is **ontological rather than relational** would help, but this needs to be stated with care to avoid circularity.

---

### 2. Challenge from non-contrastive ontologies

Some metaphysical positions explicitly deny that reality fundamentally contrasts with non-being, for example:

* Absolute monism
* Certain strands of Neoplatonism
* Some interpretations of Parmenidean or Spinozist metaphysics

These views can claim that:

* Reality is fully actual and lacks an internal contrast with non-being.
* “Not being the case” is not a genuine alternative state.

Such positions would reject the MFC not out of idleness, but because they deny that non-obtaining is a coherent comparator at the fundamental level.

The MFC treats this as collapse; proponents of these views will treat it as **misapplied contrastivism**.

---

### 3. The charge of conceptual necessity vs. metaphysical necessity

Critics may argue that the MFC establishes a constraint on *the concept* of facthood, not on reality itself. On this view:

* The principle shows how “fact” must function to be intelligible.
* It does not follow that reality itself must satisfy that constraint independently of conceptualization.

The proposal explicitly denies this move, but the denial requires further argument. Otherwise, deflationists can accept the MFC as a **conceptual norm** while rejecting its metaphysical force.

---

### 4. Bootstrap vulnerability

The claim that rejecting the MFC “dissolves metaphysics” is rhetorically strong but dialectically risky. A critic can reply:

* Metaphysics need not preserve factivity.
* Its task may be descriptive, expressive, or structural rather than contrastive.

Such a response does not refute the MFC, but it reframes metaphysics in a way that sidesteps the constraint rather than engaging it directly.

---

## Overall assessment

The Minimal Factivity Constraint is:

* **Internally consistent**
* **Genuinely minimal**
* **Well-motivated**
* **Effective against vacuous and purely stipulative ontologies**

Its main vulnerability is not incoherence, but **scope**: whether every serious metaphysical position must accept contrastive facthood as a condition on being.

If the aim is to rule out metaphysics that deny any distinction between actuality and non-actuality, the MFC succeeds. If the aim is to compel *all* metaphysics to accept contrast as fundamental, further argument is required against non-contrastive monisms and deflationary reinterpretations.

In short: the MFC is a strong candidate for a minimal constraint on ontological seriousness, but it functions as a **philosophical filter**, not a neutral axiom that every metaphysical framework is forced to accept on pain of contradiction.
