Distinction-Space is the open-ended field of admissibility fixed by the fact that anything can count as something only insofar as distinctions are in play that exclude alternatives. It is exhausted by distinctions and the constraint relations among them, and is non-closed in principle because any determinate configuration of distinctions can itself be further distinguished. What appear as entities, laws, modalities, or worlds are stabilized patterns or coarse-grainings within this same space. A partition is a local restriction within distinction space: a selection of which distinctions are treated as relevant for a context, model, or perspective, thereby introducing locality and determinacy without altering admissibility itself. Partitions therefore come in degrees of restrictiveness, but a completely unrestricted “partition” collapses into distinction space as such and is no partition at all—it is simply the absence of relevance restriction.

Distinction-space is combinatorial in its constitutive role: any distinction is intelligible only insofar as it can co-apply, exclude, or constrain other distinctions, and structure consists entirely in these patterns of joint applicability and constraint. This combinatorial character is not that of a fixed state space, but of an open-ended field in which distinctions are always available to be taken together in determinate ways. It is globally symmetric with respect to contrastability—no configuration of distinctions forecloses the possibility of being distinguished from an alternative or mirror configuration—even though it is locally asymmetric with respect to constraint, since distinctions can restrict admissible continuation in non-reciprocal ways within a context. Crucially, this combinatorial role is possible only because distinctions are discrete at the level of applicability: a distinction applies or does not apply, with no intermediate state that would not itself require further distinction. Continuity and gradation are therefore admissible only as patterns articulated through discrete distinctions. The space remains non-closed—no total combination can be fixed—but whatever structure appears does so through discrete, combinable distinctions and the asymmetric constraints they impose on one another.
