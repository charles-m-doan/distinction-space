# Against the Being / Intelligibility Distinction

- Standard metaphysical pictures presuppose a gap between *being* and *intelligibility*:
  - being is taken to be what is,
  - intelligibility is taken to be what can be meaningfully articulated about what is.

- The appearance of this gap depends on the assumption of a determinate ontological remainder:
  - something that is what it is independently of all distinction,
  - yet is still taken to underwrite contrasts between intelligible and non-intelligible.

- This assumption cannot be sustained under the constitutive conditions of intelligibility:
  - to posit a gap requires a contrast between two sides,
  - sustaining a contrast requires determinacy,
  - determinacy requires predication,
  - predication requires application conditions,
  - application conditions require distinction.

- A purported “being as such” that is prior to all distinction therefore lacks application conditions:
  - it cannot be characterized positively,
  - it cannot be characterized negatively,
  - it cannot ground a contrast,
  - and it cannot play the role required to sustain a gap.

- Appeals to ineffability, inaccessibility, or transcendence do not avoid this result:
  - treating being as ineffable still treats it as determinately *that which* is ineffable,
  - which reintroduces illicit determinacy without distinction.

- The being/intelligibility gap is thus not bridged but eliminated:
  - not because intelligibility captures or exhausts being,
  - but because the idea of a determinate being that stands apart from all distinction has no coherent application conditions.

- The collapse of this gap is foundational:
  - subsequent eliminations (fundamentality, governing laws, explanation-terminating roles) are downstream expressions of the same illicit remainder.
