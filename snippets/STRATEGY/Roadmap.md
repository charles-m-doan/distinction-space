# Distinction Constraint — Plan of Action

---

## PHASE 0 — Fixed Constraints

- DC is not a theory, framework, ontology, or interpretation.
- DC is a constitutive constraint on intelligibility.
- Eliminations target explanatory roles, not entities or models.
- Ontology is treated strictly as a layer-relative abstraction.
- No semantic → ontological slide.
- No subject, convention, or substitute fundamentality introduced.

---

## PHASE 1 — Formal Paper Construction

### 1. Introduction
- Classify the contribution (constitutive, eliminative).
- State evaluation criteria (internal coherence, eliminative adequacy).
- Fix scope and non-aims.
- No history, no citations.

---

### 2. Context and Non-Alignment (1–2 pages)
**All references are confined to this section.**

#### 2.1 Historical Orientation (Minimal)
- Kant: conditions of possibility without subject/faculty grounding.
- Wittgenstein: limits of sense without quietism.
- Carnap: framework-relative ontology without permissive conventionalism.
- One short paragraph per figure.
- One citation per figure.
- Explicit overlap and explicit break.

#### 2.2 Contemporary Diagnostic Context
- Cite citable overviews only (e.g. SEP entries, review papers).
- Topics: fundamentality, laws of nature, realism, effective theories.
- Purpose: acknowledge instability of fundamentality claims.
- Explicit statement of non-participation in these debates.

#### 2.3 Explicit Non-Alignment
- Not Kantian.
- Not Wittgensteinian.
- Not Carnapian.
- Not semantic deflationism.
- Not grounding metaphysics.
- Not structural realism.

---

### 3. Foundations: Constitutive Conditions of Intelligibility
- Pure argument. No citations. No historical or contemporary discussion.
- Articulate distinction, applicability, and determinacy conditions.
- Show that the supposed gap between being and intelligibility has no coherent application conditions.

---

### 4. The Distinction Constraint
- Formal statement of the constraint.
- Framed negatively (what roles are blocked).
- Introduced once.

---

### 5. Eliminations (Role by Role)
- Fundamental ontology.
- Governing laws.
- Explanation-terminating primitives.
- Grounding as depth substitute.
- Each section:
  - Identify the role.
  - Show incompatibility with DC.
  - No replacement offered.

---

### 6. Ontology as Abstraction Layer
- Ontologies as coarse-grainings of distinction-space.
- Utility preserved.
- Fundamentality denied.

---

### 7. Implications Boundary
- Explicit non-claims:
  - No model selection.
  - No physical constants determined.
  - No discreteness or computation privileged.
  - No contingent facts derived.

---

### 8. Formal Appendix (Optional)
- Isabelle proof (sanity check).
- Labeled as encoding, not grounding.
- Purpose: The formal appendix does not justify the Distinction Constraint; it makes explicit the dependency structure of its eliminative consequences and shows that no further assumptions are doing the work.
